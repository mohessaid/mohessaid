I am not really a fan of how the available projects/tasks managing apps are. Which is why when I first thought of my project Ta9in, the first thing I built in my mind was a different project/tasks manager application. In fact, the main project and another one are built on top of that manager application.

## Why Though?

I worked with Jira for almost ten years. I used Asana, ClickUp, Bitrix24, FreedCamp, etc. I used GitHub Projects as well. Trello is one of these. I even used Notion to track some of my projects. I don't want to delve into the realm of ToDo list apps.

All these apps are not designed for the way I think about work. They impose a structure that doesn't fit my brain. They are designed for teams, not individuals. They are designed for PMs, not engineers.

## What I Want

A tool that:

1. Doesn't get in the way
2. Lets me think in terms of outcomes, not tasks
3. Has a fast keyboard-driven interface
4. Doesn't require me to maintain it — it should adapt to how I work

## The Idea

The core idea is to build a project manager around a single concept: **context switching has a cost**. Everything in the tool should minimize that cost. Group related work. Surface what matters now. Hide what doesn't.

More on this as the idea matures.
