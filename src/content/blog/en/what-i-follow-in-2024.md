Around 7:00 a.m. today, I checked LinkedIn out of curiosity. I stumbled on a post where Bassem Dghaidi shared his list of recommended books and channels. I wanted to share what I find worthy of following. The list kept growing, so here it is as a post.

## YouTube Channels

For YouTube, I only subscribe to channels that meet these criteria:

1. Their content interests me.
2. They don't over-post.
3. They have a unique style.

### [Fireship](https://youtube.com/fireship)

You know it. Follow it.

### [No Boilerplate](https://www.youtube.com/@NoBoilerplate)

I subscribed for his Rust videos. He has one named "Rust for the Impatient." He talks about other things too.

### [AAarthificial](https://www.youtube.com/@aarthificial)

Raw talent. He is the creator of [Motion Canvas](https://motioncanvas.io).

### [Andrej Karpathy](https://www.youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy)

The ChatGPT guy. Machine Learning content.

### [Sebastian Lague](https://www.youtube.com/@SebastianLague)

Great content on Math and Game Dev.

### [CodeAesthetic](https://www.youtube.com/@CodeAesthetic)

Hidden gems.

### [Dreams of Code](https://www.youtube.com/@dreamsofcode)

He talks DevOps better than most specialized channels, and he's a generalist.

## For Fun

### [Honeypot](https://www.youtube.com/@Honeypotio)

Great documentaries about software history.

### [Green Code](https://www.youtube.com/@Green-Code/)

You can't get enough of his content.

### [FaceDevStuff](https://www.youtube.com/@FaceDevStuff)

Super funny, talking about programming.
